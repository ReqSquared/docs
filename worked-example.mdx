This is what the engine produces for a single requisition across the context lenses that met the configured `top_n` threshold.

## The Requisition

Requisition `RIQ-0014`. Currently a filled req from the observation period. Compared against historic population fills in the relevant peer groups.

## The Output

Four metrics evaluated across the context lenses that qualified for this requisition.

### applicants_total = 89

| Context ID | Z-Score | Mean | SD | Survival Range |
|------------|---------|------|-----|----------------|
| org_UK_Tech_Engineering_Entry | 1.11 | 50 | 36 | 19 to 77 |
| org_UK_Tech_Engineering | 0.52 | 67 | 42 | 27 to 99 |
| org_UK | 0.48 | 69 | 41 | 38 to 94 |
| company_Tech_Engineering_Entry | 1.15 | 52 | 32 | 28 to 69 |
| company_Tech | 0.58 | 64 | 43 | 25 to 96 |

89 applicants is above the mean across every lens. The most specific lens (`org_UK_Tech_Engineering_Entry`) flags this as 1.11 standard deviations above its peer group — the **Watch** signal threshold.

The company-wide Entry-level Tech Engineering lens shows similar deviation (1.15) — confirms the pattern isn't just an org artifact.

### days_to_first_screen = 15

| Context ID | Z-Score | Mean | SD | Survival Range |
|------------|---------|------|-----|----------------|
| org_UK_Tech_Engineering_Entry | -0.39 | 18 | 8 | 11 to 24 |
| org_UK | -0.22 | 17 | 7 | 10 to 22 |
| company_Tech_Engineering_Entry | -0.07 | 16 | 8 | 7 to 21 |

15 days to first screen is faster than the peer group means. All three context lenses show negative z-scores (lower is better for this metric per the schema's `direction: -1`).

The pattern is consistent across the three qualifying lenses. The recruiter is screening early for this kind of req.

### percent_reviewed_in_x_days = 62%

| Context ID | Z-Score | Mean | SD | Survival Range |
|------------|---------|------|-----|----------------|
| org_UK_Tech_Engineering_Entry | 0.44 | 47% | 34% | 15% to 80% |
| org_UK | 0.55 | 42% | 35% | 14% to 68% |
| company_Tech_Engineering_Entry | 0.91 | 33% | 31% | 8% to 53% |

62% reviewed within the configured time window is above the mean for every context lens (higher is better per `direction: 1`).

The strongest signal is against `company_Tech_Engineering_Entry` (z = 0.91) — this requisition is meaningfully better at quick review than its company-wide peer group. The org-level lenses are closer to neutral but still positive.

### time_to_fill = 62

| Context ID | Z-Score | Mean | SD | Survival Range |
|------------|---------|------|-----|----------------|
| org_UK_Tech_Engineering_Entry | -0.92 | 68 | 7 | 62 to 72 |
| org_UK | 0.20 | 58 | 23 | 39 to 76 |
| company_Tech_Engineering_Entry | -1.48 | 74 | 8 | 68 to 79 |
| company_Tech | 0.16 | 60 | 15 | 47 to 70 |

This is where context becomes the entire thesis.

62 days to fill scores:

- **-0.92** against `org_UK_Tech_Engineering_Entry` (faster than UK Tech Engineering Entry-level)
- **+0.20** against `org_UK` (slightly slower than UK overall)
- **-1.48** against `company_Tech_Engineering_Entry` (much faster than company Tech Engineering Entry-level)
- **+0.16** against `company_Tech` (slightly slower than company Tech overall)

Same requisition. Same metric value. Four different signals.

The most specific lenses show this requisition closed *faster* than its narrow peer group. The broader lenses show it closed roughly *on pace* with the wider population.

Both are true. Both are useful.

## What the Output Says

`RIQ-0014` reads as a strong requisition:

- Higher applicant volume than peer group (good if the role is competitive)
- Faster screening turnaround (good)
- Higher review rate than peer group (good)
- Filled faster than its specific peer group, on pace with the broader population (good)

The composition across lenses tells the story. The engine doesn't grade. It surfaces.

## What the Operator Does

The operator reads the table and decides:

- *Is this a strong req or did we get lucky?* — patterns across multiple lenses help answer
- *Should we look at this recruiter's other reqs?* — recruiter context is the entry point
- *Is the high applicant volume a feature or a problem?* — depends on operational context
- *Should we replicate the screening cadence elsewhere?* — the consistent negative `days_to_first_screen` across lenses suggests yes

The Descriptive System produces the evidence. The operator does the synthesis.

## What This Output Does Not Do

This output does not:

- Score the recruiter
- Rank the requisition
- Predict future performance
- Recommend an action

The output is descriptive position relative to peer group across multiple lenses. The interpretation is the operator's. The decision is the operator's.

Decision Systems consume this kind of descriptive output as input. They compose multi-lens signals into operational decisions (the Air Traffic Controller composition does this). The Descriptive System sits one architectural layer below the decision-making.
