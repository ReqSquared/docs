BlueCube is the methodology framework for Talent Acquisition decision intelligence, built on composable analytical primitives that produce explainable Health Scores on every requisition.

BlueCube combines structured methodology authoring, composable analytical cubes, and ATS-native consumption — all powered by a published cube specification.

The methodology framework that combines structured analytical cubes, defensible composition logic, and operationally legible output — all built on tool-agnostic primitives that run alongside your existing ATS.

It empowers different personas across your TA function:

- **TA Leaders:** can navigate operational decisions with consistent decision intelligence, getting trusted signal on every open requisition without dashboard interpretation work.
- **Recruiters:** can prioritize their portfolio with plain English explainability, understanding why a req is flagged through stacked methodology rather than opaque AI scoring.
- **Hiring Managers:** can self-serve through native ATS filtering on the BlueCube Health Score. No requests to analytics required.

## How is BlueCube different?

At the foundation of BlueCube's methodology framework is a published cube specification — the shared architectural pattern that enables both methodology authors and TA operators to work with trusted, consistent analytical outputs.

The cube specification provides the governed methodology foundation that makes operational decision intelligence possible. It organizes analytical work from disparate methods into centralized, consistent definitions that compose reliably into Health Scores. Without the cube specification, methodology work would struggle with inconsistent metrics, scattered analytical logic, and ungoverned composition — making outputs unreliable and potentially indefensible.

## Explainable Composition

  ![Blue Cube Wiring1](/images/blue_cube_wiring2.png)
</Frame>

Unlike vendor AI hiring tools, BlueCube cubes don't operate as black boxes. Instead, every cube produces three explicit outputs through the three-output contract — creating a transparent composition architecture. The composition layer acts as the methodology between analytical inputs and TA decisions — every Health Score must compose through this documented logic, which surfaces every contributing signal to make the score auditable.

The **three-output contract** specifies that **every cube produces:**

1. Dataframe
2. Decision
3. Explainability

This architecture lets machine learning model builders leverage the full power of established analytical and statistical methods while maintaining operational legibility, combining sophistication with full transparency.

Explainability is enforced structurally at the methodology layer, ensuring consistent transparency across all cube outputs.
