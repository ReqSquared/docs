## Peer Group Voting System

RequisitionIQ Level 1 is the Descriptive System for the requisition lifecycle. It takes one filled requisition and compares it against its peer group across multiple context lenses — recruiter, organization, company — using z-scores, survival percentiles, and company goal thresholds.

The same req gets a different signal depending on which lens you look through. That is the point.

## What Level 1 Is

Level 1 is **descriptive statistics**. Not predictive. Not prescriptive.

The engine produces signals. What you do with them is yours.

A requisition can score *faster than this recruiter's normal* against the recruiter context and *slower than the company* against the company context. Both are true. Both are useful.

## Where Level 1 Sits

Within RequisitionIQ, Level 1 is the foundational layer:

- **Level 1 — Descriptive.** Position relative to peer group. Multiple context lenses. Statistical signals.
- **Higher Levels — Decision Systems.** Cubes composing into operational outputs. The Air Traffic Controller composition lives in [Decision Systems](/decision-systems/air-traffic-controller).

Level 1 describes where things sit. Decision Systems decide what to do about it.

## The Five-Step Pipeline

The engine runs five steps, top to bottom:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Build the observation dataframe">
    Current quarter reqs tagged with the schema's analytical signals.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Build sample population dataframes">
    Prior quarter fills grouped by context lens. Sample size per config `top_n` threshold.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Expand observations across context lenses">
    Each requisition multiplied across every valid groupby combination — recruiter × country × job_group × job_family × job_level and every broader subset.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Z-score each observation">
    Compare each req's metrics against its sample population group's mean and standard deviation.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add survival percentiles">
    Cumulative distribution position within the sample population group.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Each step produces a named, inspectable dataframe. Nothing is hidden.
